What Are Whales?

Whales are fully aquatic mammals, part of the infraorder Cetacea. They have evolved to live entirely in water, breathing air through blowholes located on top of their heads.

Unlike fish, whales are warm-blooded, give live birth, and nurse their young with milk. Their streamlined bodies and powerful tails make them agile swimmers.

Whale Anatomy

  • Blowhole: Allows whales to breathe at the surface without lifting their heads fully out of water.
  • Flippers and Flukes: Help with steering and propulsion.
  • Layer of Blubber: Insulates against cold ocean waters.
  • Sensory Systems: Including echolocation in toothed whales for navigation and hunting.
