Whales

Discover the fascinating world of the largest marine mammals

What are Whales?

Whales are a diverse group of fully aquatic placental marine mammals. They are an informal grouping within the infraorder Cetacea, usually excluding dolphins and porpoises.

Types of Whales

  • Baleen Whales: These whales have baleen plates for filtering food from water. Examples include the Blue Whale, Humpback Whale, and Gray Whale.
  • Toothed Whales: These whales have teeth and feed on fish, squid, and other marine animals. Examples include the Sperm Whale, Orca (Killer Whale), and Beluga.

Interesting Facts

  • The Blue Whale is the largest animal ever known to have existed, reaching lengths of up to 100 feet (30 meters).
  • Whales communicate using complex vocalizations, some of which can travel long distances underwater.
  • Many whale species undertake long migrations between feeding and breeding grounds.
  • Whales play a crucial role in marine ecosystems by helping regulate the food chain and nutrient cycling.
