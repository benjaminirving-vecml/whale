Introduction

Blue whales (Balaenoptera musculus) are the largest animals to have ever lived on Earth, reaching lengths of up to 100 feet (30 meters) and weights of up to 200 tons.

Physical Characteristics

Blue whales have long, streamlined bodies with a bluish-grey coloration and lighter underside. Their massive size is supported by a thick layer of blubber that insulates them in cold ocean waters.

Habitat and Distribution

They are found in all the world's oceans, from the polar regions to the tropics, migrating seasonally between feeding and breeding grounds.

Diet

Blue whales primarily feed on tiny shrimp-like animals called krill. During feeding season, a single adult can consume up to 4 tons of krill per day using their baleen plates to filter them from seawater.

Conservation Status

Once hunted to near extinction, blue whales are now protected worldwide. Although populations are slowly recovering, they remain endangered due to threats like ship strikes, entanglement in fishing gear, and climate change.
